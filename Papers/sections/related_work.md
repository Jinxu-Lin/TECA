# Related Work

## Knowledge Editing in Language Models

Knowledge editing methods modify specific factual associations in pretrained language models without full retraining. ROME \citep{meng2022locating} performs rank-one updates to MLP weight matrices at a critical layer identified by causal tracing, treating factual knowledge as key-value associations in a linear associative memory. MEMIT \citep{meng2022mass} extends this to multi-layer distributed editing for batch fact insertion. More recent approaches include PMET \citep{li2024pmet}, which operates on both attention and MLP modules, and constrained fine-tuning methods \citep{zhu2020modifying}. While these methods achieve high editing success rates, they implicitly assume that the parameter-space directions they modify correspond to where knowledge is ``stored'' --- an assumption that the localization-editing disconnect \citep{hase2023does} calls into question. Knowledge neurons \citep{dai2022knowledge} provide a complementary perspective by identifying individual neurons associated with factual knowledge, though at a different granularity than the subspace-level analysis we pursue. Our work examines the editing assumption directly by measuring whether ROME's editing directions share geometric structure with independently computed attribution directions.

## Training Data Attribution

Training data attribution methods estimate the influence of individual training examples on model predictions. Influence functions \citep{koh2017understanding} approximate leave-one-out effects via the inverse Hessian, but scale poorly and exhibit fragility on deep networks \citep{basu2021influence}. TRAK \citep{park2023trak} projects gradients to a lower-dimensional space for computational tractability. Recent work has revealed significant limitations: \citet{wang2024influence} document failure modes of influence functions on LLMs, while the fundamental reliance on retrain-based ground truth remains an unresolved evaluation challenge. In the subspace approach, MDA \citep{kwon2024mda} decomposes influence into subspace components, and RIF \citep{tang2025rif} shows that influence function accuracy depends critically on effective dimensionality. Our work takes a different perspective --- rather than improving attribution methods, we characterize the geometric properties of attribution gradients and their relationship to an independent knowledge probe (editing).

## Knowledge Localization and the Localization-Editing Disconnect

Causal tracing \citep{meng2022locating} identifies MLP layers causally responsible for factual predictions by measuring the effect of activation corruptions and restorations. \citet{hase2023does} demonstrated that knowledge localization does not reliably predict editing success --- facts localized to specific layers can often be edited at other layers, and vice versa. This disconnect has no satisfactory explanation at the parameter level. Subsequent work has explored alternative localization methods \citep{geva2023dissecting} and representation-level analysis \citep{li2024inference}, but none has connected the localization-editing gap to the geometric structure of parameter space. We note that our work addresses a related but distinct question: Hase et al.\ study whether \textit{localization predicts editing success} (a behavioral question), while we study whether \textit{editing and attribution directions share geometric structure} (a geometric question). The connection is that both findings point to different knowledge operations accessing different parameter-space structures.

## Geometric Analysis of Neural Network Parameter Space

Geometric perspectives on neural network parameters have yielded insights in several domains. Loss landscape analysis \citep{li2018visualizing} characterizes the curvature of the loss surface. Mode connectivity \citep{draxler2018essentially} and linear mode connectivity \citep{frankle2020linear} study the geometric structure of optima. In the context of language models, representation geometry has been studied through probing \citep{hewitt2019structural} and geometric properties of embeddings \citep{ethayarajh2019contextual}. However, the geometry of parameter-space directions associated with specific knowledge operations has not been studied. Our six-component framework provides a systematic geometric characterization of this previously unexplored relationship.

## Unified Perspectives on Knowledge Operations

Recent surveys \citep{zhang2025unified} have attempted to unify knowledge editing and attribution within a common framework, but focus on methodological comparisons rather than geometric relationships. Infusion \citep{guu2025infusion} connects influence functions to parameter-space optimization, and SOURCE \citep{pruthi2024source} provides improved training gradients via unrolled differentiation. MAGIC \citep{zhao2025magic} demonstrates that attribution quality varies dramatically with retrieval methods, motivating our attribution quality analysis. None of these works examines whether the parameter-space directions produced by different knowledge operations are geometrically commensurable --- the central question of our study.
